About Rudraksha

Rudraksha is also called dried stone. These stones serve as prayer beads. The word Rudraksha means the eyes of Lord Shiva. There are different types of Rudraksha. It has 1 to 21 faces on its body. Face means Mukh. This is the seed of a tree in the Himalayan zone. Meat has covered the seed. Each seed is known as a bead. 5 Mukhi Rudraksha are available in the market. These are the secret beads of Hindus, Buddhists, and Sikhs.
Types
Rudraksha is available in broadly 26 different types from 1-21 mukhi, Gaurishankar, Trijuti, Savar Naag, Ganesh & Garb Gauri Rudraksha each differing in the number of grooves or mukhis on the surface or the shape of the bead. These varieties are available in both Nepal as well as Java trees.
